\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Thailand Market Study\
\
## Overview\
Thailand represents an ideal proof-of-concept (POC) market for Veyya\'92s entry into Southeast Asia\'92s fast-growing on-demand home and beauty services sector.  \
Its combination of urban density, digital adoption, and tourism-driven service demand creates an opportunity for high-frequency, repeatable transactions with limited competition in the premium space.\
\
---\
\
## Market Size and Growth\
\
- **SEA home services market:** Projected to reach **$2.2T (2023\'962028)**.\
- **Thailand market size:** Approx. **$5B**, growing at **15\'9620% annually**.\
- **Urban, mobile-first population:** ~52% of the population lives in cities; Bangkok leads in smartphone penetration (>90%).\
- **Digital payments adoption:** Over 80% of urban users use PromptPay or e-wallets.\
- **Top verticals:** Beauty, cleaning, pet care, wellness, and basic home maintenance.\
\
### Key Drivers\
1. Rising dual-income households demanding convenience.\
2. Post-pandemic acceptance of in-home services.\
3. Growing number of freelancers seeking flexible, independent income.\
4. Government focus on SME digitization and tourism-related service enhancement.\

## Legal & Regulatory Context\
\
### Entity Formation\
- **Recommended structure:**  \
  - **HoldCo:** Singapore (for fundraising and IP ownership).  \
  - **OpCo:** Thailand (for operations, compliance, local contracts).\
\
### Licensing Requirements\
- Service businesses must register under the Department of Business Development (DBD).  \
- Thai nationals or entities must hold **51% ownership** in most service categories unless BOI approval or special exemption applies.  \
- Foreign founders can operate under the **Foreign Business License (FBL)** or via a Thai partner.\
\
### Labor & Freelancer Framework\
- Freelancers are legally allowed to operate under **self-employed contracts**.\
- Contractors must handle their own tax filings (Personal Income Tax 5\'9610% bands).\
- No work visa required for Thai citizens; foreign providers need a Non-B Visa + work permit.\
\
### Data Privacy\
- **PDPA (Personal Data Protection Act)** governs data use and consent.\
- Veyya\'92s app must ensure opt-in consent, secure data handling, and deletion upon request.\
\

## Taxation & Financials\
- **Corporate tax (Thailand):** 20%.\
- **VAT:** 7%.\
- **Withholding tax (freelancers):** 3\'965%.\
- **Dividends repatriated to Singapore:** subject to 10% WHT (can be reduced via DTA).\

## Market Entry Strategy\
\
### Why Thailand First\
1. Founding partner with local insights and network.\
2. Favorable digital behavior and influencer ecosystem.\
3. Cost-effective marketing through micro-influencers and partnerships.\
4. Government initiatives supporting SME digitization.\
5. Proximity to other ASEAN markets (Malaysia, Singapore, Vietnam).\
\
### Target Launch Cities\
- **Bangkok** \'96 high density, digital adoption.\

- **Chiang Mai** \'96 growing expat and remote-worker population.\
