\f0\fs24 \cf0 # Freelancer Model and Legal Framework (Thailand)\
\
## Overview\
Veyya\'92s freelancer model is designed to remain compliant with Thai labor laws while providing flexibility, fair compensation, and protection to service professionals.  \
Freelancers will operate as **independent contractors**, retaining autonomy over their schedules while being integrated into the platform\'92s training and quality ecosystem.\
\
---\
\
## Legal Context\
\
### Self-Employment in Thailand\
- Freelancers in Thailand are legally recognized as **independent contractors** under the Civil and Commercial Code.\
- No employer\'96employee relationship is created if:\
  1. The worker controls their own hours and methods.\
  2. The platform only facilitates job matching and payments.\
  3. No fixed wages or benefits (salary, leave) are provided.\
\
### Key Compliance Principles\
- All freelancers must **sign a service agreement** defining them as independent providers.\
- Veyya must **not issue employee payslips** or deduct social security.\
- Freelancers handle their own **personal income tax filings** (5\'9610% band).\
- The company must **withhold 3\'965% tax** per transaction and remit it to the Thai Revenue Department.\
\

## Contractor Agreement Outline\
\
### 1. Engagement Scope\
Freelancers (\'93Service Providers\'94) agree to deliver on-demand beauty, cleaning, or home services to customers via the Veyya platform.\
\
### 2. Relationship\
- The Provider is **not an employee** of Veyya.\
- No entitlement to employment benefits, insurance, or paid leave.\
- Provider may accept or decline bookings at their discretion.\
\
### 3. Payment\
- Providers receive **70%** of the booking value.\
- Payments made weekly or bi-weekly via bank transfer or e-wallet.\
- Veyya withholds **3\'965%** tax per Thai Revenue requirements.\
\
### 4. Standards and Compliance\
- Providers must maintain service ratings above 4.2/5.\
- Must comply with PDPA for customer data confidentiality.\
- Must complete training and safety induction before activation.\
\
### 5. Termination\
- Either party may terminate with written notice.\
- Breach of policy or misconduct leads to suspension or delisting.\
\
### 6. Liability\
- Providers are responsible for their own tools and conduct.\
- Optional micro-insurance covers service-related accidents.\

\
